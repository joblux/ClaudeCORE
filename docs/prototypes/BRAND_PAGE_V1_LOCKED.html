Company Profile

Cartier is a French luxury jeweller and watchmaker, founded in Paris in 1847.

The house rose to prominence supplying European royal and imperial courts in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries — an era that earned it the description of the jeweller of kings and the king of jewellers. It is known both for fine jewellery and for some of the earliest purpose-built wristwatches, the Santos and the Tank among them.

Run by the Cartier family until 1964, the Maison has been part of the Swiss group Compagnie Financière Richemont since 1988. Today it operates more than two hundred boutiques worldwide and remains one of the defining names in high jewellery and watchmaking.

The origin
LC
Louis-François Cartier
1819 – 1904 · Founder

He founded the Maison in Paris in 1847, taking over the jewellery workshop of his master, Adolphe Picard. Born in Paris to a family of modest means, he trained as a jeweller before establishing the business that would carry his name. Under the Second Empire the firm drew court patronage, with Princess Mathilde among its earliest clients from 1856. In 1874 he passed the house to his son Alfred — whose sons Louis, Pierre and Jacques would later establish Cartier across Paris, London and New York.
